Introduction

What is DevOps?

DevOps (an acronym for development and operations) is a set of practices that brings together software development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops).
Its goal is to shorten the software development lifecycle and provide continuous delivery of high-quality software.

DevOps complements agile software development practices, as many of its principles are derived from Agile methodologies.

The key feature of the DevOps movement is its active advocacy for automation and monitoring at every step of the software build process, from integration, testing, deployment, to infrastructure management.

DevOps aims for shorter development cycles, more frequent deployments, and more reliable releases, all closely aligned with business objectives.

Why is DevOps Important?

DevOps is critical in today’s fast-paced tech environment because it enables faster and more reliable software releases. By automating processes, teams can focus on higher-value tasks, like improving customer experiences and reducing operational risks.

With continuous integration and delivery, development teams can catch issues early, ensuring that software updates happen seamlessly without downtime or major disruptions.

Furthermore, the alignment between development and operations ensures that the software not only works in theory but performs as expected in real-world environments.
